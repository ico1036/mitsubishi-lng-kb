 01 — Three theses , read backward from the data

 Ⅰ HEDGE 

 The value chain is not a margin engine — it is a geopolitical hedging instrument .

 Mitsubishi bought into U.S. shale upstream — Aethon, ~$5.2bn — in the very month the Hormuz crisis peaked (Jan 2026). That timing reveals the real function: equity stakes and tolling rights let the house swing supply to self-sourced volumes when a chokepoint freezes. Fifty years of accumulation pays as profit in peacetime and as a shield in crisis.

 ▲ Dual function · revenue in calm, armor in conflict

 Ⅱ MOAT 

 What Buffett would hold forever isn't LNG — it's the cash-flow predictability integration manufactures.

 The verified record shows not a single-resource bet but a portfolio flattened by upstream-to-downstream diversification — precisely the Buffett-style moat of durable, predictable cash flow. The paradox the data exposes: Korea has no listed vehicle — public utility or trading house — that packages this same integrated cash-flow profile.

 ⚠ Evidence gap · Buffett rationale unverified — see graph

 Ⅲ LIMIT 

 Korea's "copy Mitsubishi" strategy only works halfway .

 SK E&S and POSCO International's vertical integration cuts volume -disruption risk — but per IEEFA's core finding, it cannot stop a global price shock (JKM doubling, GDP −3% scenarios). "Value chain = escape from energy shocks" is closer to myth. The true lever is not securing molecules but hedging price through contract structure.

 ⚠ Half-protection · supply security ≠ price security
